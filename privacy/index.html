Privacy Policy

Last updated:

Tools Pack provides client-side utility tools that run in your browser. Unless a tool explicitly says otherwise, your inputs are processed locally on your device and are not sent to our servers.

1) Overview

  • No accounts required. You can use the site without creating an account.
  • Local processing first. Most tools work entirely in your browser (HTML/JS/Web APIs).
  • Minimal metrics. We may collect aggregate, non-identifying usage statistics to keep the site reliable and improve it.

2) Data we process

  • Tool inputs. Text, numbers, files, and settings you enter into a tool. By default these stay in your browser tab and are lost when you close it, unless you export or save.
  • Local storage. Some tools (e.g., notes, preferences, recent files list) may save small items to localStorage on your device so they persist across visits. You can clear this at any time.
  • Technical diagnostics. For uptime and performance we may record aggregated events such as page views, approximate device type, anonymized IP (or truncated IP), error codes, and response times.
  • Server logs. Our hosting provider may keep standard web logs (e.g., timestamp, requested URL, user-agent, truncated IP) for security and debugging.

3) Files you upload

Where a tool needs a file (e.g., image converter), it is handled in your browser when possible. If a feature requires remote processing, we will state that in the tool UI. Files used for remote processing are transmitted securely, processed, and—unless stated otherwise—deleted automatically after completion.

4) Cookies & similar tech

  • Essential cookies. Used to deliver the site, prevent abuse, or remember minimal preferences.
  • Preference storage. Tool settings may be stored via localStorage (not sent to us).
  • Analytics/ads cookies. See sections below for details and your choices.

5) Analytics

We may use lightweight, privacy-respecting analytics to understand overall usage (which pages are popular, performance, referrers). We do not build profiles or sell personal data. Where possible, we avoid storing full IP addresses.

6) Advertising

We may show ads (e.g., Google AdSense). Ad partners may use cookies or device identifiers to deliver, limit, and measure ads, and—if you allow—personalize them. You can opt for non-personalized ads where offered.

Learn how Google uses data with partner sites: policies.google.com/technologies/partner-sites

7) Legal bases (EEA/UK)

  • Contract/legitimate interests: to deliver and secure the service, prevent abuse, and fix problems.
  • Consent: for non-essential cookies/ads personalisation where required by law. You can withdraw consent at any time.

8) Your choices & controls

  • Browser controls: block/clear cookies, site data, and localStorage from your browser settings.
  • Export & delete: tools that save data locally usually offer export/clear options; you can also clear site data in your browser.
  • Ads settings: manage ad personalisation in your Google account or use “Ad Personalization Off”.
  • Do Not Track: we honor browser privacy features where feasible.

9) Data retention

  • Local data: remains on your device until you clear it or uninstall the browser.
  • Server logs: kept for a limited period for security/diagnostics, then deleted or anonymized.
  • Remotely processed files (if any): deleted after processing unless clearly stated otherwise.

10) Security

We use industry-standard protections (HTTPS, access controls, least-privilege). No method is 100% secure, but we aim to minimize collected data and keep most processing on your device.

11) International transfers

Hosting and providers may operate globally. Where required, we use safeguards (e.g., SCCs) for data transfers.

12) Children’s privacy

Our site is not directed to children and we do not knowingly collect personal information from children. If you believe a child has provided personal data, please contact us and we’ll remove it.

13) Changes to this policy

We may update this notice as our service evolves or to meet legal requirements. The “Last updated” date shows the latest version. Material changes will be highlighted where practical.

14) Contact us

Questions or privacy requests? Email: you@example.com

15) Definitions (quick)

  • LocalStorage: browser feature that stores small data on your device only.
  • Personal data: information that identifies or can reasonably identify a person.
  • Processing: any operation performed on data (collecting, storing, using, deleting, etc.).
