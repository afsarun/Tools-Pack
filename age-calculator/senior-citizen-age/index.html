Senior Citizen Age

A person is considered a senior citizen when they reach a specific age, usually 60 or 65 years, depending on the country and official government rules.

At What Age Does One Become a Senior Citizen?

In most countries, a person becomes a senior citizen between the ages of 60 and 65. Some benefits may start earlier at age 55, but official government recognition usually begins later.

Official Senior Citizen Age

The official senior citizen age is defined by government policy. This age determines eligibility for pensions, healthcare programs, and other public benefits.

Senior Citizen Age in the United States

In the United States, a person is generally considered a senior citizen at 65 years old, which aligns with Medicare eligibility. Some discounts and programs may begin at age 55 or 62.
